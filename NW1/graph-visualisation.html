    Visualising graphs NW1

    Learning Objectives

    1 Visualise NetworkX graphs using `nx.draw()` and related drawing functions.
    2 Apply different layout algorithms such as `spiral_layout`, `spring_layout` and `random_layout` to control node positioning.
    3 Customise node and edge appearance including colour, size, shape and labels.
    4 Create subgraphs and combine network visualisation with other plots for integrated interpretation.
    5 Overlay centrality measures onto network diagrams to enhance structural insight.

    Introduction

    Visualisation plays a crucial role in understanding network structure, revealing patterns that may not be obvious from numerical summaries alone. In this section, you will learn how to draw and customise graphs using NetworkX and matplotlib, experiment with different layout algorithms and highlight important nodes based on centrality measures. By combining quantitative analysis with clear visual representation, you will develop a more intuitive understanding of how structure and influence are distributed within a network.

    Summary

    In this section, we explored how to visualise networks using NetworkX and matplotlib, translating structural information into intuitive graphical representations. We applied different layout algorithms, including spring layout, spiral layout, spectral layout, and random layout, to examine how node positioning influences interpretation. We customised node appearance by adjusting colour, size, shape, and labels, and visualised subgraphs to focus on biologically relevant regions of a network. By integrating visualisation with previously calculated centrality measures, we strengthened our ability to interpret structural patterns and communicate insights from complex biological networks.
